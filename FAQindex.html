  • Product

  • What's the flight time of the Pegasus?

    The Pegasus can fly for 35 minutes on a single charge.

    We have been experimenting with a variety of different payload capacities. We aim for the Pegasus to carry roughly 2.2 lbs or 1 kg. However, other heavy duty models will be released in the future adding payload capacity and range.

  • What kind of weather can the Pegasus handle?

    The Pegasus is water resistant and can fly in 40+ mile per hour winds.

  • Functionality

  • How do I send a package?

    Sending a package is as easy as:

    • Downloading the mobile app.
    • Loading your item into the cargo bay.
    • Selecting a friend to send to.
    • Clicking “deliver”!

  • How do I control the Pegasus?

    There are two ways to control the Pegasus:

    • Mobile app. Our mobile app allows you to initiate deliveries and perform autonomous waypoint navigation.
    • RC Remote. A remote control radio will be included for those that demand full control.

  • How do you prevent unwanted deliveries?

    The Pegasus will not takeoff unless both the sender and receiver confirm the delivery. This communication will be facilitated by our dedicated smartphone app.

  • Safety

  • How safe is the Pegasus?

    At Hylio safety is our number one priority. Sophisticated pre-flight diagnostics. The Pegasus conducts a pre-flight check and will not takeoff if battery life, payload size, and delivery distance are out of operational bounds. Active flight monitoring. The Pegasus also actively monitors itself during flight and will automatically return to home if it detects a problem. Hexacopter Design The addition of two extra motors allows continuous flight even when one or two of them go out.

  • How will the Pegasus avoid buildings and other tall objects?

    The Pegasus has a detailed internal map of its surroundings which it uses to navigate around tall objects. The Pegasus also has built-in geofences around city centers and airports and will automatically route around them.

  • Regulations

  • Is drone delivery legal? Can I make money delivering things?

    Yes! Transportation of property for compensation or hire is ALLOWED. The FAA guidelines on delivery are listed here.

  • Where can I fly?

    You can fly anywhere as long as you follow your local regulations. In the United States, flight must be more than 5 miles away from airports and at altitudes below 400 feet. More information about guidelines about where you can fly from the FAA here.

  • What are the restrictions of flying and delivery?

    The flight must be within visual line of sight of the operator. The operator cannot be in a moving vehicle or aircraft during the flight. You cannot fly above any persons not directly participating in the delivery. You must fly during daytime – or civil twilight (30 minutes before sunrise to 30 minutes after sunset)

  • I have a first person view camera, does that qualify for visual line of sight?

    No, FPV cameras or other types of technology do NOT qualify for visual-line of sight (VLOS). You must use your physical eyes to observe the flight.

  • Can someone else satisfy VLOS as a visual observer?

    Yes, as long as the flight is in visual line of sight of the visual observer.
